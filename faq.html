How to turn a photo in to a cross stitch pattern

Stitchel is a free cross stitch pattern maker that converts any photo into a printable counted cross stitch or pixel art pattern, right in your browser. Import an image and Stitchel maps each color to DMC embroidery floss, builds a stitch-by-stitch chart, calculates the finished size and thread counts, tracks your progress, and exports a PDF pattern or PNG — no signup, nothing to install.

How do I convert a photo into a cross stitch pattern?
Click Import, choose a photo, then set the pattern size in stitches and confirm. Stitchel pixelates the image and snaps each pixel to a color from your chosen palette. Switch to Pattern mode to see the full stitch chart, then Save as PDF to print it.
Where can I get free cross stitch patterns?
Make your own for free. Instead of hunting for free cross stitch patterns online, turn any photo you already have into a printable pattern with Stitchel — no purchase, no subscription, and no signup. Every pattern you create is free to download as a PDF or PNG.
Is Stitchel free?
Yes. Stitchel is completely free with no account or signup required. It runs entirely in your browser, so your photos never leave your device.
Is Stitchel good for cross stitch beginners?
Yes. Stitchel is a beginner-friendly way to start counted cross stitch. Begin with a small pattern (around 32×32) so it stitches up quickly, use the DMC Thread palette to get exact floss numbers to buy, and let the built-in Stitch Tracker keep your place as you learn.
Can I make a pattern from a photo, character, or artwork?
Yes. Stitchel turns any image into a pattern — a personal photo, a pet, a favorite character, or your own artwork. Import the image, pick a size, and Stitchel builds the chart automatically. Please only stitch copyrighted characters for your own personal use.
What is DMC floss, and how does color matching work?
DMC is the most widely used brand of cross stitch embroidery thread, with each shade identified by a number. Pick the DMC Thread palette and Stitchel matches every color in your image to the closest real DMC floss number, so you can buy the exact threads your pattern needs.
Can I build my own color palette?
Yes. Click the + next to the palette selector to create a custom palette. Add colors by DMC number or hex code, name the palette, and reuse it across projects.
What size should I make my pattern, and can Stitchel calculate it?
Size is measured in stitches. Smaller patterns (around 32×32) stitch up quickly and suit simple images; larger patterns (100×100 or more) capture more detail but take longer. You can set any size between 16 and 600 stitches on import, and Stitchel acts as a cross stitch calculator — it works out the finished dimensions and the thread count you'll need for each color.
Can I track my stitching progress?
Yes. In Pattern mode, the built-in Stitch Tracker lets you mark stitches complete and shows how many remain, so you can pick up where you left off.
Can I use Stitchel on my phone?
Yes — Stitchel works on mobile for importing a photo and previewing your pattern. For the full editor with drawing tools, a computer works best.
How do I export or print my pattern?
Use Pattern then Save as PDF for a printable chart with a color-to-DMC key, or PNG to export the pixel art as an image.
