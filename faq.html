How to turn a photo in to a cross stitch pattern

Stitchel is a free, in-browser tool that converts any photo into a printable cross stitch or pixel art pattern. Import an image and Stitchel maps each color to DMC embroidery floss, builds a stitch-by-stitch chart, tracks your progress, and exports a PDF pattern or PNG — no signup, nothing to install.

How do I convert a photo into a cross stitch pattern?
Click Import, choose a photo, then set the pattern size in stitches and confirm. Stitchel pixelates the image and snaps each pixel to a color from your chosen palette. Switch to Pattern mode to see the full stitch chart, then Save as PDF to print it.
Is Stitchel free?
Yes. Stitchel is completely free with no account or signup required. It runs entirely in your browser, so your photos never leave your device.
What is DMC floss, and how does color matching work?
DMC is the most widely used brand of cross stitch embroidery thread, with each shade identified by a number. Pick the DMC Thread palette and Stitchel matches every color in your image to the closest real DMC floss number, so you can buy the exact threads your pattern needs.
Can I build my own color palette?
Yes. Click the + next to the palette selector to create a custom palette. Add colors by DMC number or hex code, name the palette, and reuse it across projects.
What size should I make my cross stitch pattern?
Size is measured in stitches. Smaller patterns (around 32×32) stitch up quickly and suit simple images; larger patterns (100×100 or more) capture more detail but take longer. You can set any size between 16 and 600 stitches on import.
Can I track my stitching progress?
Yes. In Pattern mode, the built-in Stitch Tracker lets you mark stitches complete and shows how many remain, so you can pick up where you left off.
Can I use Stitchel on my phone?
Yes — Stitchel works on mobile for importing a photo and previewing your pattern. For the full editor with drawing tools, a computer works best.
How do I export or print my pattern?
Use Pattern then Save as PDF for a printable chart with a color-to-DMC key, or PNG to export the pixel art as an image.
